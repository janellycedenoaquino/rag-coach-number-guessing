STRATEGY: Hard Mode Survival Guide (1-100, 5 attempts)

Hard mode is unforgiving. Binary search needs up to 7 guesses to guarantee a win on a 1-100 range, but you only have 5. This means you must play nearly perfectly and use proximity hints to your advantage.

OPTIMAL OPENING SEQUENCE:
Guess 1: 50 (midpoint of 1-100)
Guess 2: 25 or 75 depending on hint (midpoint of remaining half)
Guess 3: midpoint of remaining quarter
Guess 4: midpoint of remaining eighth
Guess 5: your best remaining guess

This gives you a winning range of about 6 numbers by guess 5 — manageable with proximity hints.

USE PROXIMITY TO SKIP STEPS:
If you get 🔥🔥🔥 Super hot (within 3) early, don't keep bisecting — scan those 3 numbers directly.
If you get 🔥 Warm (within 10), tighten your range to ±10 around your guess immediately.

WHAT NOT TO DO:
- Do not open with anything other than 50. Any other opening wastes your first guess.
- Do not guess sequentially (51, 52, 53...). You will run out of attempts.
- Do not let the "Cold" hint demoralize you on guess 1 — that just means the secret is more than 20 away, which is expected when your range is 100 numbers wide.

MINDSET:
Hard mode rewards calm, methodical play. Every guess must eliminate as much of the range as possible. Trust the math over your instincts.
